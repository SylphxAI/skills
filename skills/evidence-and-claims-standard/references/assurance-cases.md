# Assurance Cases

Use an assurance case when a high-impact claim needs an auditable argument
rather than a flat evidence list.

1. State one top-level claim with exact subject and boundary.
2. Decompose it through explicit argument strategies into falsifiable
   subclaims.
3. Record contexts, assumptions, justifications, and declared defeaters.
4. Attach primary evidence to the narrowest subclaim it supports.
5. Leave unsupported or defeated nodes open; do not average them into a green
   score.
6. Re-evaluate affected ancestors when evidence or assumptions change.

The artifact is an **Assurance Case**: claim, strategy, subclaim, context,
assumption, evidence, defeater, and unresolved-gap nodes. A diagram is optional;
the semantic graph is required.

Research basis: Goal Structuring Notation community standard:
<https://scsc.uk/gsn>.
