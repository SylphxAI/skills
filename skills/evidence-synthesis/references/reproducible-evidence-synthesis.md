# Reproducible evidence-synthesis method

Use a review design proportionate to the question. A systematic review seeks a
comprehensive, predeclared, reproducible answer. A rapid review narrows or
streamlines methods transparently. A scoping review maps evidence and gaps
rather than estimating one effect. A structured review may be appropriate when
the evidence is heterogeneous or outside research databases, but it must still
preserve protocol, provenance, exclusions, and uncertainty.

## Protocol template

```text
Question and decision use:
Review type and rationale:
Population/system/context:
Intervention/exposure/comparator/outcomes, if applicable:
Time, geography, language, source-type, and publication-status boundaries:
Sources and exact search strategies:
Inclusion and exclusion criteria:
Deduplication and screening method:
Extraction fields and quality/risk-of-bias method:
Dependence, conflict, and heterogeneity handling:
Qualitative or quantitative synthesis method:
Certainty method and sensitivity analyses:
Protocol deviations and update trigger:
```

## Evidence table minimum

```text
Source ID / citation / persistent locator
Underlying study or dataset identity
Design and collection period
Population, system, or operating context
Intervention/exposure and comparator
Outcome definition and measurement time
Estimate/result and uncertainty
Risk-of-bias or quality judgment with reasons
Directness and applicability
Funding, conflicts, limitations, and dependencies
Supports, contradicts, or is neutral to each synthesis claim
```

Do not impose medical-study hierarchies on engineering logs, legal materials,
qualitative research, or operational records. Select domain-appropriate quality
criteria and explain them. Across domains, retain the same invariants:
traceability, declared selection, source dependence, measurement validity,
counterevidence, uncertainty, and bounded claims.

## Authoritative methods

- PRISMA, **PRISMA 2020 Statement and Checklist** — transparent reporting of
  why a review was done, what was done, and what was found:
  <https://www.prisma-statement.org/prisma-2020-statement>
- Cochrane, **Cochrane Handbook for Systematic Reviews of Interventions** —
  question formulation, searching, study selection, bias assessment,
  synthesis, heterogeneity, and interpretation:
  <https://training.cochrane.org/handbook/current>
- Campbell Collaboration, **Writing a Campbell systematic review** — protocol,
  conduct, reporting, and publication resources for social and policy
  questions:
  <https://www.campbellcollaboration.org/research-resources/writing-a-campbell-systematic-review.html>

PRISMA is a reporting guideline, not a guarantee of methodological quality.
Cochrane and Campbell methods are domain-shaped; reuse their reproducibility
principles without pretending their eligibility or certainty rules are
universal.
