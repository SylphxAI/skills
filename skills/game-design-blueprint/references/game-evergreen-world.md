# Game Evergreen World Fairness

## 1. Objective

Create one durable game history in which a new, returning, or low-population player can reach meaningful play, social contribution, and fair competition within a declared baseline-access time bound without optional paid acceleration, while veterans retain valuable breadth, mastery, identity, creations, relationships, history, and status. Baseline access may itself be a premium purchase or subscription.

Do not solve world age by repeatedly opening fresh progression/economy servers and abandoning old cohorts. That fragments friends, content, liquidity, operations and trust, teaches players that durable investment expires, and turns economic reset into acquisition cadence.

## 2. Separate world identity from physical topology

```text
logical world/account identity:
durable progression/economy/social authority:
regional placement for latency, law and resilience:
dynamic instances/channels for capacity:
matchmaking pools and cross-region policy:
data residency and migration:
cross-save/cross-play/account portability:
```

`evergreen-1` — Regions, shards, instances and channels may exist for latency, legal isolation, resilience or capacity. They must not manufacture a recurring economic/progression fresh start by default.

`evergreen-2` — Prefer a shared logical identity, social graph, entitlements and progression semantics with dynamic routing. If authority requires separate regions, define portable identity/value and an explicit merge/migration contract.

`evergreen-3` — Never market infrastructure scaling as a new world that invalidates earlier participation unless the product is explicitly a finite-run world with informed consent and durable metaprogression.

## 3. State classes

Classify every important state:

| Class | Examples | Rollover/reset rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Durable ownership | paid items, earned collection, account unlocks, creations, identity, history, relationships | preserve; migrate/compensate if semantics change |
| Durable breadth/mastery | skills, sidegrades, knowledge, professions, exploration, mentorship reputation | preserve; horizontal extension preferred |
| Seasonal competitive | rank, ladder rating, tournament qualification | may reset with disclosed seeding, history and rewards |
| Renewable world control | territory, seasonal structures, contested resources | may cycle under fair participation/anti-zerg rules |
| Ephemeral event | temporary objectives, event instances/currency | explicit start/end, conversion, claims and compensation |
| Operational | instance placement, channel, queue, cache | invisible and freely rebalanced without changing earned value |

`state-1` — A season can renew goals and competition without erasing durable earned/purchased value.

`state-2` — Before any reset/rollover define history, seeding, unclaimed rewards, purchases, temporary inventory, leaderboard records, guild/territory state, conversion, compensation and late-entry treatment.

## 4. Power and participation architecture

### Bound vertical dominance

Use a contextual combination of:

- reachable vertical cap and diminishing returns;
- stat/power compression across expansions/world age;
- gear/level normalization or bracketed competitive modes;
- dynamic level/power sync in lower content;
- sidekick/mentor/party sync for mixed-progression groups;
- loadout/loaner/role baselines for eligible modes;
- skill-, decision- and coordination-based mastery after viability;
- horizontal progression: sidegrades, roles, traversal, crafting, expression, collection, housing, creation, prestige, history.

Late joiners should not need to buy relief from a deliberately unbounded veteran gap.

### Reachability SLO

Declare as a product hypothesis:

```text
cohort and starting state:
baseline-access active/elapsed time to core competence without optional paid acceleration:
time to viable build/loadout:
time to known-friend co-play without carrying/boosting:
time to useful guild/community contribution:
time to normalized competitive eligibility:
content/story prerequisites and skip/recap:
confidence interval, guardrails and validation:
```

Do not use a universal time. Measure actual new, returning, disabled/access-needs, low-skill, low-connectivity, and different-locale cohorts.

## 5. Catch-up without veteran invalidation

Catch-up function can use:

- accelerated obsolete vertical progression while keeping current mastery meaningful;
- world-age or distance-to-viability multipliers with caps and anti-abuse;
- recap, guided goals, curated loadouts and returning-player state repair;
- access to relevant old content through scaling, archive/rotation and group fill;
- deterministic essentials before optional randomness;
- mentor rewards based on newcomer success/agency, not carry volume;
- account-wide horizontal unlocks and shared convenience;
- event/season entry baselines and normalized competitive kits.

`catchup-1` — Catch-up closes access to viable participation; it does not grant top prestige, erase veteran history, or make churn the best progression strategy.

`catchup-2` — Calculate eligibility from authoritative history and distance to current viability. Cap transfers, boosts and multi-account exploitation; preserve an appeal path for false positives.

`catchup-3` — Never sell the only practical catch-up path after designing an excessive gap.

## 6. Social fairness and population resilience

### Guild/community design

- contribution roles useful before endgame power;
- mentor/apprentice matching and shared goals with anti-farming rules;
- novice-friendly discovery and protected recruitment;
- mixed-progression party scaling and reward fairness;
- guild catch-up, inactive leadership recovery and succession;
- anti-zerg/anti-monopoly mechanics for territory and scarce resources;
- cross-world friends/guilds where authority permits;
- reporting, block/mute, moderation and newcomer harassment protection.

### Low-population fallback

Use, as genre permits:

```text
cross-region/cross-shard pools with latency rules
-> asynchronous competition/cooperation
-> ghosts/replays/agent or bot fill disclosed appropriately
-> dynamic encounter scaling
-> known-friend/private play
-> schedule aggregation and queue forecasts
-> mode degradation or rotation
```

Opening a new permanent economy to hide low liquidity is not a fallback. Preserve progress and explain degraded/rotated modes.

## 7. Economy longevity

Model new, returning, regular, skilled, payer/non-payer, wealthy veteran, guild, trader and manipulator profiles across world ages.

Track:

- price index, currency velocity, source/sink balance and stockpiles;
- wealth/item concentration and mobility;
- essential/competitive item affordability and time-to-viability;
- market liquidity, spreads, manipulation, bot/RMT/fraud and guild monopolies;
- expansion/event faucets, retired content and item obsolescence;
- taxes/fees/consumption/repair/crafting/collection/prestige sinks;
- refund/chargeback/duplication/support adjustments and ledger reconciliation.

`economy-1` — Use bounded sinks and value-preserving conversions, not surprise confiscation or total reset.

`economy-2` — Prevent zero-sum newcomer exclusion: essential resources need renewable/non-monopolizable paths, instancing or catch-up supply where the design requires them.

`economy-3` — Automatically adjust admitted source/sink/catch-up parameters inside fairness/economy envelopes; major value semantics and paid/earned ownership changes require new exact candidates and compensation analysis.

## 8. Evergreen content and seasons

- Preserve essential story/mechanics in an archive, recap, replay, rotation or alternative acquisition path.
- Keep old zones/activities meaningful through scaling, modern rewards, collections, mentorship, rotating objectives or systemic remix.
- Avoid mandatory linear completion of years of obsolete content before current social play.
- Seasons add goals, variants, world changes, narrative, competition and expression; they do not routinely wipe identity/ownership.
- Limited content declares availability, return/alternative path, rights/localization/support, purchases and shutdown behavior.
- Agent-generated content requires provenance, validation, moderation, accessibility, localization, performance and rollback before exposure.

## 9. Automated fairness controller

Observe by cohort/world age/platform/region:

```text
time-to-first-viable-build and meaningful contribution
new/returning completion and comprehension
power/wealth gap and competitive outcome after normalization
party success, carry dependence, mentor/newcomer reciprocity
guild join/contribution/retention and harassment/report outcomes
queue time, match quality, concurrency and low-population fallback use
essential-item affordability, price index, concentration and manipulation
content reachability, archive usage and prerequisite abandonment
veteran breadth/mastery/status value and perceived invalidation
```

Policy actions inside immutable floors:

- adjust guided path, recap, catch-up rate/cap, sync, bracket, loaner kit, matchmaking pool, encounter scaling, content rotation, mentorship and admitted economy controls;
- increase cross-region/asynchronous/bot fallback or rotate modes under low liquidity;
- halt abusive transfers/markets or risky economy expansion;
- never auto-open a permanent fresh economy, erase durable value, change committed odds, or let a revenue model rewrite fairness floors.

Use shadow evaluation, simulation, canary, maximum step, hysteresis/cooldown, cohort stickiness, audit/replay, independent watchdog and surface-specific rollback/compensation.

## 10. Required simulations and playtests

Simulate and then validate with real players:

| Entrant | Compare against |
| --- | --- |
| day-0 new player | day-0 population and mature-world veterans |
| day-30 new player | established social/economy cohorts |
| year-1 new player | expansion/endgame/wealth concentration |
| returning player | changed systems, decayed guild, expired events |
| baseline-access player without optional paid acceleration | typical and high-spend paths |
| guildless/solo/known-friend player | organized guild and public matchmaking |
| low-population region/time | peak cross-region population |
| accessibility/low-end/poor-network cohort | baseline supported cohort |

Test reachability, comprehension, agency, fairness, fun, economy, social acceptance, veteran value, harassment, support load and abuse. Synthetic simulations cannot prove perceived fairness or social belonging.

## 12. Research routes

Reachable or source-verified on 2026-07-11; examples demonstrate mechanisms, not universal solutions:

- [Guild Wars 2 Dynamic Level Adjustment](https://wiki.guildwars2.com/wiki/Dynamic_level_adjustment) — official game wiki describes downscaling higher-level players in lower-level areas while retaining meaningful rewards; a reference for mixed-progression content.
- [Guild Wars 2 Mastery](https://wiki.guildwars2.com/wiki/Mastery) — official game wiki describes account-wide post-cap horizontal ability progression; a reference for veteran breadth beyond vertical level growth.
- [EVE Online: A Single-Shard Sandbox](https://www.eveonline.com/news/view/eve-online-a-single-shard-sandbox) — first-party route for long-lived shared-world identity; its economy and player experience are not automatically suitable elsewhere.
- [Elder Scrolls Online: One Tamriel](https://www.elderscrollsonline.com/en-us/news/post/25859) — first-party route for world-scaling/access changes; validate current accessibility and transfer limits.
