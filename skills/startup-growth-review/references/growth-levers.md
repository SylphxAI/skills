# Growth Levers

## Positioning

- `growth-positioning-1` — Name the enemy: what bad old way does the product replace?
- `growth-positioning-2` — Identify the narrow beachhead user before broad market claims.
- `growth-positioning-3` — Make the value prop concrete: time saved, risk reduced, revenue unlocked, quality improved, or status gained.
- `growth-positioning-4` — Align proof with buyer risk: demos for product risk, case studies for outcome risk, security/compliance docs for enterprise risk.

## Landing pages

- `growth-landing-1` — Above the fold should answer: who it is for, what changes, why believe, what to do next.
- `growth-landing-2` — Replace feature lists with job-based outcomes when the buyer is not yet educated.
- `growth-landing-3` — Use CTAs that match readiness: demo, try, join waitlist, read docs, compare, calculate.
- `growth-landing-4` — Put objections near the decision point, not in a hidden FAQ only.

## Activation

- `growth-activation-1` — Define the first value moment in product behavior, not account creation.
- `growth-activation-2` — Remove setup work that can be inferred, imported, or deferred.
- `growth-activation-3` — Use templates and sample data only when they teach the real workflow.
- `growth-activation-4` — Ask for permissions at the moment of need.

## Retention

- `growth-retention-1` — Identify the recurring job that brings the user back.
- `growth-retention-2` — Make progress visible across sessions.
- `growth-retention-3` — Trigger lifecycle messages from real product events, not arbitrary calendar delays.
- `growth-retention-4` — Improve collaboration loops when the product becomes more valuable with team usage.

## Pricing and packaging

- `growth-pricing-1` — Match packaging to the value metric customers already understand.
- `growth-pricing-2` — Avoid hiding the path from free to paid; users should know what success will cost.
- `growth-pricing-3` — Gate advanced scale, compliance, or collaboration features rather than first value.
- `growth-pricing-4` — Enterprise pricing needs procurement proof: security, DPA, support, SLA, and admin controls.

## Acquisition

- `growth-channel-1` — Start where buyer intent already exists before creating a new habit.
- `growth-channel-2` — Turn product artifacts into distribution when possible: public pages, badges, reports, templates, benchmarks.
- `growth-channel-3` — Build comparison and migration pages for markets with incumbent pain.
- `growth-channel-4` — Use founder-led content to teach the category, not to narrate company activity.
