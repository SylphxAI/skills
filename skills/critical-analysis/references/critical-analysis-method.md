# Critical Analysis Method

## Evidence ledger

Start with four columns:

| Item | Type | Quality | Effect on analysis |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Directly observed fact | observation | source, date, revision | constrains hypotheses |
| Interpretation | inference | reasoning and alternatives | updates ranking |
| Required premise | assumption | testability and sensitivity | may invalidate conclusion |
| Missing fact | unknown | acquisition cost and value | may justify targeted research |

Do not let inference migrate into the facts column through repetition.

## Competing-hypothesis matrix

Use when causes or explanations compete:

| Evidence or expected observation | Hypothesis A | Hypothesis B | Hypothesis C |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Observation 1 | consistent | inconsistent | neutral |

Prioritize diagnostic evidence: observations that differ across hypotheses.
Evidence compatible with every hypothesis adds little. Try to disprove the
leading hypothesis rather than collecting only confirmations.

Do not force independence where causes can coexist. Distinguish mutually
exclusive explanations from contributing factors.

## Challenge methods

Choose only those suited to the stakes:

- **Key-assumptions check:** list premises that must hold, rank sensitivity,
  and test the most fragile premise.
- **Premortem:** assume the chosen conclusion or plan failed; identify credible
  causes and leading indicators.
- **Devil's advocate:** construct the strongest evidence-based objection to
  the leading answer.
- **Team A/Team B:** independently build the strongest cases for materially
  different interpretations.
- **Red team:** model how a system, policy, or argument fails under the declared
  threat and operating environment.
- **Alternative futures:** explore distinct future conditions only when they
  change the robust action now.

Independent agents are useful when assigned different hypotheses, evidence
sources, or failure models. Asking several similar agents the same open-ended
question often creates correlated agreement rather than independent proof.

## Calibration

Prefer probability ranges or verbal levels with explicit meaning over false
precision. State:

- confidence in the factual premises;
- confidence that the considered hypotheses cover the material space;
- confidence in the conclusion conditional on those premises; and
- the observation most likely to move the conclusion.

Do not convert “we found no alternative” into certainty. Record the bounded
search performed.

## Stop rule

Stop analysis when additional evidence has low expected value: it is unlikely
to change the conclusion, action, or risk treatment relative to its acquisition
cost and delay. Continue when a cheap discriminating observation could reverse
a material conclusion.

## Research basis

This method synthesizes:

- the CIA's structured techniques for information quality, assumptions,
  competing hypotheses, devil's advocacy, Team A/Team B, red teams, and
  alternative futures:
  <https://www.cia.gov/resources/csi/static/Tradecraft-Primer-apr09.pdf>
- empirical evidence that mechanically applying Analysis of Competing
  Hypotheses can produce mixed results and should not be treated as an oracle:
  <https://doi.org/10.1002/acp.3550>
- premortem research on prospective hindsight:
  <https://doi.org/10.1002/bdm.3960020103>
- research on probabilistic calibration and superforecasting:
  <https://doi.org/10.1177/1745691615577794>
- CRITIC's use of external feedback during model self-correction:
  <https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.11738>
- evidence that unsupported intrinsic self-correction can degrade answers:
  <https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.01798>
