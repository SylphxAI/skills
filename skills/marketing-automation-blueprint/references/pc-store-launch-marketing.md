# PC Store Launch Marketing

Use for the audience and communication program around a Steam or other PC-store
release. Store Listing owns page narrative, tags/metadata and conversion tests;
Product Asset Production owns exact capsules, screenshots, trailer and press-kit
files; Distribution owns build/store/release proof; Launch Readiness owns the
cross-domain verdict and first-week health gates.

## Launch funnel and truth inputs

```text
game/product promise, genre/category and target player:
exact demo/playtest/release identities and availability:
positioning and claim-proof IDs:
store listing revision and exact asset-pack IDs:
price/discount/DLC/territory authority and support truth:
wishlist, demo, creator, press, community and launch objectives:
```

Treat the funnel as:

```text
qualified_impression -> store_understanding -> wishlist_or_follow
-> demo/playtest_or_evidence_beat -> purchase -> first_session
-> retained_play_or_use -> authentic_review_or_advocacy
```

A wishlist is useful only when a declared beat gives the audience a reason to
return: demo, festival, dev update, creator proof, launch date, feature proof or
meaningful community event.

## Program rules

- `pc-launch-mkt-1` — Capsule, short description, trailer, screenshots, tags
  and creator language express the same genre/job promise; route page-order and
  tag decisions to Store Listing and exact files to Product Asset Production.
- `pc-launch-mkt-2` — Demo/playtest communication teaches the core loop quickly,
  sets final-product expectations, records scope/known limits and carries a
  useful wishlist/follow next action.
- `pc-launch-mkt-3` — Creator keys and embargoes bind exact build, known-issues
  note, press kit, content/disclosure guidance, support owner, timing and
  revocation/correction path. Never send unstable or unowned keys.
- `pc-launch-mkt-4` — Community hub, moderation, bug-report format, known-issue
  state, support escalation and patch-note cadence have owners before traffic.
- `pc-launch-mkt-5` — Price, discount, DLC, refund and review statements use
  current platform/commercial authority; a campaign cannot invent policy or
  imply incomplete base-product value.
- `pc-launch-mkt-6` — If store promise and build differ, pause promotion and
  route a listing/asset correction or product fix before buying more traffic.
- `pc-launch-mkt-7` — Separate valid defect themes from coordinated abuse.
  Moderation/reporting never replaces fixing crashes, performance, saves,
  onboarding, settings, controller, server or claim mismatch.
- `pc-launch-mkt-8` — Public incident language distinguishes investigating,
  identified, fix in validation, hotfix live, rollback live and roadmap
  clarification; do not promise timing without release authority.

## Calendar skeleton

| Window | Marketing decision | Required handoff |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Early page | positioning, audience, wishlist reason and proof cadence | listing revision and initial asset pack |
| Demo/festival/playtest | acquisition sources, learning questions, end-card/action, creator/community beat | exact build/scope, support and feedback routes |
| Pre-launch | creator/press list, embargo, localized kit, release/price messaging | locked build, known issues, policy and rights evidence |
| Launch day | coordinated owned/earned/paid communication and status route | Distribution live readback and Launch Admission |
| Days 1–3 | defect/expectation communication, patch-note amplification, promotion hold/resume | release-health decision and exact note |
| Days 4–7 | retained-value, refund/review/support and campaign learning | first-week closeout and positioning/campaign hypotheses |

## Measurement and response

Segment store visit, wishlist/follow, demo start/completion, campaign/source,
creator coverage, purchase, first-session, retained outcome, refund reason,
review theme and support issue by exact build, locale/region and relevant
hardware/input context. Attribution is not incrementality; reserve holdouts or
use bounded triangulation where feasible.

Use owner-approved targets. A generic threshold may be proposed only as a
clearly labeled hypothesis, never platform policy or a public promise. Promotion
holds respond to claim mismatch, release-health stop, broken deep links,
unavailable territory/price, unsafe community state, expired rights, excessive
complaints/refunds or absent support—not merely lower click-through rate.

Return the launch calendar, audience/message/claim map, wishlist/demo program,
creator/press/community contracts, support and incident communication paths,
measurement/countermetrics, activation/hold/recovery states, and exact handoffs
to Listing, Assets, Distribution and Launch Readiness.
