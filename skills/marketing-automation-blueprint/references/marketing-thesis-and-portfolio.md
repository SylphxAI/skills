# Marketing Thesis And Portfolio

## 1. Truth inputs

Marketing starts from exact artifacts:

```text
product promise and user job:
claim-proof registry:
features/platforms/locales actually available:
price, offer, entitlement and fulfillment owners:
release/version/deep-link state:
audience, age, territory, consent and exclusion policy:
support/refund/safety truth:
```

If a fact is unavailable, create a typed handoff request or omit the claim. Never turn a hypothesis into public proof.

## 2. Marketing thesis

```text
For <audience with intent/lifecycle state>, communicate <proof-backed change>
through <channel mechanism> at <context/cadence>, leading to <valuable action>
and <retained outcome>, disproved by <guardrail or causal result>.
```

Map message hierarchy:

1. category/job and who it is for;
2. changed user outcome;
3. distinctive mechanism;
4. substantiated proof;
5. next action and accurate expectation;
6. exclusions, price/renewal, eligibility or material limitations.

`thesis-1` — Marketing translates product truth for an audience; it does not own product truth.

`thesis-2` — A channel is valuable when it reaches the right intent with a suitable message and produces incremental retained value, not because a leader uses it.

## 3. Audience and lifecycle model

Avoid one static persona. Model decision-relevant state:

```text
job/intent and awareness:
problem/category/product knowledge:
new | evaluating | activated | retained | payer | collaborator | creator
at-risk | lapsed | returned | refunded | disputed | suppressed:
platform/locale/territory/device:
age/sensitive-category/consent:
channel permission and fatigue:
product eligibility and release availability:
```

For each audience edge define trigger, expected value, claim/message, channel, frequency, exclusions, outcome, countermetrics, expiry, and holdout.

`audience-1` — Do not infer vulnerability, health, finance, age, or other sensitive states for persuasion beyond lawful, consented, purpose-limited use.

`audience-2` — Suppression is a first-class state: unsubscribed, opted out, minor/ineligible, complaint, refund dispute, recent conversion, over-frequency, unavailable product, stale identity, or unsafe context.

## 4. Complete channel capability sweep

| Capability | Role | Required integration |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Website/landing | canonical public explanation and conversion | claims, release, performance, accessibility, analytics |
| SEO | capture declared search intent through useful content | canonical URLs, structured data, internal links, anti-spam |
| AEO/AI discovery | machine-readable, citable product truth | claims/evidence, structured content, freshness |
| Store/ASO | convert qualified store traffic | listing specialist, localized assets, release truth |
| Organic social | distribute useful stories, proof, releases and community artifacts | platform adapters, provenance, replies, fatigue |
| Content/editorial/video | educate, demonstrate and build durable discovery | topic graph, rights, YouTube/web/social transforms |
| Community | reciprocal support, identity and participation | moderation, escalation, product feedback, no fake activity |
| Creator/influencer/affiliate | trusted audience translation | fit, disclosure, rights, fraud, incremental measurement |
| PR/launch | concentrated news and category narrative | verifiable news, assets, availability, support |
| Partnerships | co-distribution and integrated value | contracts, claims, tracking, customer/support boundary |
| Referral/invite | product-native person-to-person value | referral artifact, recipient value, fraud/reversal |
| Cross-promotion | portfolio-fit discovery | product surface contract, disclosure, deep link, holdout |
| Lifecycle CRM | timely return/education/support | consent, user state, notification/email/in-app owners |
| Reputation/reviews | authentic public proof and response | review-feedback artifact, platform-specific policy |
| Paid search/social/video/display | bounded demand capture/creation | channel adapter, spend policy, conversion/causal evidence |
| Retargeting where permitted | reconnect known intent | strict consent, exclusions, frequency, sensitive-data floor |
| Marketing analytics | reconcile exposure, cost, outcome and incrementality | event/cost ledger, identity, dedupe, privacy |

Build the adapters and operating capability for the full sweep. Activate only where product truth, audience value, consent, platform authority, measurement and economics pass.

## 5. Portfolio states

For every capability:

```text
Construction: build-to-scale-now | queued-by-exact-dependency | floor-blocked | retired
Proof: hypothesis | implementation-verified | scale-verified | production-proven
Activation: dormant | shadow | canary | active-bounded | scaled | held | paused | withdrawn
```

Never use `not relevant` without a user/channel mechanism explanation. Never use an inactive campaign as evidence that adapter construction can be deferred.

## 6. Handoffs

- Product design supplies promise, claims, product surfaces, deep links, age/territory and release semantics.
- Product lifecycle supplies platform/release/global asset/SDK evidence.
- Campaign specialist supplies one campaign/offer state machine.
- Referral specialist supplies qualification/grant/reversal/fraud.
- Review-feedback specialist supplies platform solicitation and feedback/reputation policy.
- Distribution/listing specialists supply store eligibility, metadata and live evidence.
- Marketing returns audience/message/channel/creative manifests, demand evidence, attribution/incrementality, and product feedback without overwriting upstream truth.

## 7. Review checklist

- Every message traces to a claim-proof and release artifact.
- Audience state includes consent, exclusions, fatigue and product eligibility.
- Full capability construction is separate from activation and spend.
- Channel roles are distinct enough to avoid duplicated reach and self-competition.
- The objective is incremental retained value/contribution with trust and quality floors.
- Specialist artifacts are referenced, not copied.
