# Product Positioning Patterns

## Positioning components

| Component | Question | Failure mode |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Segment | Who feels the pain most? | Everyone language |
| Job | What progress do they seek? | Feature list |
| Alternative | What do they use now? | Pretending no competition exists |
| Category | How should buyers understand it? | Inventing a category too early |
| Differentiator | Why this product? | Generic speed/ease claims |
| Proof | Why believe it? | Unsupported superlatives |
| Fit boundary | Who is it not for? | Bad-fit churn |

## Rule IDs

- `positioning-1` — Start with the sharpest high-pain segment before expanding to adjacent users.
- `positioning-2` — Name the current alternative, including spreadsheets, manual work, agencies, or doing nothing.
- `positioning-3` — Choose a category that helps buyers compare and trust the product.
- `positioning-4` — Differentiation must map to a buyer-valued outcome, not internal architecture alone.
- `positioning-5` — Proof should match maturity: demo, benchmark, customer quote, case study, community, or founder credibility.
- `positioning-6` — Messaging hierarchy should answer: what it is, who it is for, why it matters, why now, why trust.
- `positioning-7` — Product-led pages need a concrete first action; sales-led pages need risk reducers and qualification.
- `positioning-8` — Developer products must show technical truth quickly: docs, API, CLI, examples, limits.
- `positioning-9` — Games and consumer products should position around emotion/core loop, not only feature systems.
- `positioning-10` — Positioning should exclude bad-fit users explicitly enough to reduce churn and support drag.

## Positioning decision table

| Situation | Better positioning move |
| --- | --- |
| Many competitors sound similar | Narrow segment or unique workflow proof |
| Product is new category | Anchor to familiar alternative, then name difference |
| Feature-rich but unclear | Pick primary job and relegate secondary features |
| Enterprise buyers hesitate | Lead with risk, security, support, ROI, integration proof |
| Consumer app lacks trust | Show product reality, reviews, privacy, pricing, cancellation |

## Output artifacts

Produce: positioning statement, headline options, subheadline, proof stack, objection map, bad-fit boundary, and channel-specific message notes.
