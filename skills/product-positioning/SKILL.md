# Product Positioning

Use this skill to turn a product from a list of features into a market argument.

## Workflow

1. Identify target segment, urgent job, alternatives, category frame, and proof assets.
2. Read `references/product-positioning-patterns.md`.
3. Build positioning from audience, problem, alternative, differentiator, outcome, and proof.
4. Stress-test against competitors, skeptical buyers, and wrong-fit users.
5. Produce messaging hierarchy and landing/sales usage.

## Guardrails

- Do not claim uniqueness without a defensible difference.
- Do not broaden the audience until the wedge is strong.
- Do not confuse tagline writing with positioning.

## Output format

```text
Audience:
Category frame:
Primary alternative:

Positioning:
- For: <segment>
- Who need: <job/pain>
- We are: <category>
- Unlike: <alternative>
- We provide: <differentiator/outcome>
- Proof: <evidence>

Messaging hierarchy:
- Hero:
- Proof:
- Objections:
```
