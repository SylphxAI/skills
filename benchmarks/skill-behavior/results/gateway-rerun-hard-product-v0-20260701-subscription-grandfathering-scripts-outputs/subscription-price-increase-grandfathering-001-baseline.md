## Subscription Price Increase Plan (B2B SaaS)

### 1. Objectives and Guardrails
**Goals**
- Increase ARR while protecting logo retention and NRR.
- Standardize legacy pricing and reduce grandfathered-plan complexity.
- Preserve trust through advance notice, clear value framing, and migration options.

**Guardrails**
- No surprise mid-term increases for fixed annual contracts unless contract permits.
- Honor enterprise MSAs and renewal notice requirements.
- Cap high-risk account increases where churn or downsell risk is material.
- Track impact separately from expansion/contraction to preserve clean revenue reporting.

---

## 2. Customer Segmentation and Pricing Actions

| Segment | Action | Timing | Notes |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Monthly self-serve | Increase at next billing cycle after notice | 30–45 days’ notice | Offer annual lock-in before increase |
| Legacy annual contracts | Apply at renewal | 60–90 days before renewal | No mid-term change unless contract allows |
| Enterprise customers | Apply through renewal negotiation | 90–180 days before renewal | AE-led, based on contract terms |
| Grandfathered plans | Migrate to current plan family | At renewal or chosen migration date | Offer temporary discount or phased ramp |
| Usage overages | Update overage rates prospectively | 30–90 days depending on contract | Communicate separately from base subscription |
| High-risk accounts | Exception review | Before notice | May receive cap, delay, or migration incentive |

---

## 3. Price Increase Framework

### Recommended Increase Bands
- **Standard increase:** 8–15%
- **Underpriced legacy plans:** 15–30%, phased if needed
- **Enterprise:** account-specific based on usage, value, contract terms, and benchmark pricing
- **Overages:** align to current published rates or cost-to-serve thresholds

### Caps
- Maximum first-year uplift for existing customers: **25% unless executive-approved**
- Maximum uplift for high-risk customers: **10–15%**
- No increase for customers with open critical escalations unless approved

---

## 4. Migration Offers

Offer migration paths to reduce churn and accelerate standardization.

### Available Offers
1. **Annual lock-in offer**
   - Monthly users can lock current price for 12 months by moving to annual before effective date.

2. **Phased migration**
   - Year 1: 50% of increase
   - Year 2: full current list price

3. **Plan upgrade credit**
   - Apply temporary discount when moving from grandfathered plan to current plan.

4. **Usage bundle**
   - Convert recurring overages into committed usage tier at discounted rate.

5. **Enterprise renewal package**
   - Multi-year term with capped annual uplift, expanded features, or usage commitment.

## 5. Notification Timing

| Customer Type | Minimum Notice | Owner |
|---|---:|---|
| Monthly self-serve | 30 days | Lifecycle/CS |
| Annual SMB/MM | 60 days before renewal | CS/Sales |
| Enterprise | 90–180 days before renewal | AE/CSM |
| Contractually required notice | Per MSA/order form | Legal/Sales Ops |
| Overage rate changes | 30–90 days | CS/Billing |

### Notification Sequence
1. **Internal enablement:** T-14 days before customer notice
2. **Customer notice:** T-30/60/90 depending on segment
3. **Reminder:** 14 days before effective date
4. **Final billing reminder:** 3–7 days before charge
5. **Post-change support follow-up:** 7–14 days after first invoice

---

## 6. Customer Communications

### Core Message
- Pricing is changing to support continued product investment, reliability, security, support, and platform scale.
- Existing customers receive advance notice and migration options.
- Current contracts are respected.
- Customers can contact support, CS, or their AE to discuss options.

### Email Requirements
Each notice should include:
- Current plan and price
- New plan and price
- Effective date
- Billing impact
- Overages impact, if applicable
- Migration or lock-in options
- Deadline to act
- Contact path
- Link to updated pricing terms

---

## 7. Support Scripts

### Self-Serve / Support Script
“Your subscription price is changing on [date]. The new price is [amount], and your current billing cadence remains [monthly/annual]. This update helps us continue investing in product improvements, reliability, security, and support. You can keep your current price for 12 months by switching to an annual plan before [deadline], or review available plans here: [link].”

### Grandfathered Plan Script
“Your current plan is being retired and will migrate to [new plan] at renewal/on [date]. The new plan includes [key benefits]. To ease the transition, we’re offering [discount/phased pricing] through [date].”

### Enterprise Script
“We’d like to review your upcoming renewal and align your agreement with current packaging, usage, and support needs. We have options including multi-year terms, committed usage tiers, and phased pricing.”

### Cancellation Risk Script
“I understand pricing changes can be challenging. Let’s review your usage, required features, and renewal options. Depending on fit, we may be able to offer annual lock-in, phased migration, or a right-sized plan.”

## 8. Sales and CS Exception Policy

### Exception Types
- Price cap
- Delayed effective date
- Temporary discount
- Multi-year ramp
- Grandfathered extension
- Overage bundle
- Custom enterprise packaging

### Approval Matrix
| Exception | Approval |
|---|---|
| Discount up to 10% or 3-month delay | CS/Sales Manager |
| Discount 10–25% or phased ramp | VP Sales/CS |
| Above 25%, custom terms, or legal deviation | CRO + Finance + Legal |
| Strategic account carveout | CEO/CRO approval |

### Exception Criteria
- Contractual limitation
- High churn risk
- Open support incident
- Strategic logo value
- Expansion opportunity
- Competitive displacement risk
- Poor product fit requiring right-sizing

All exceptions must be logged with reason code, financial impact, approver, and expiration date.

## 11. Revenue-Retention Reporting

Track weekly during rollout and monthly after.

**Core Metrics**
- Gross Revenue Retention impact
- Net Revenue Retention impact
- ARR uplift from price increase
- ARR lost from churn tied to increase
- Downsell attributed to price increase
- Exception ARR and discount leakage
- Customers notified
- Customers accepted
- Customers migrated
- Customers churned
- Support ticket volume
- Invoice dispute rate

### Attribution Categories
- Price increase accepted
- Price increase churn
- Price increase downsell
- Migration accepted
- Exception granted
- Contractually deferred
- Billing error
- Unrelated churn

### Audit Fields
For each account:
- Customer segment
- Original price
- New price
- Increase %
- Notice date
- Effective date
- Contract renewal date
- Exception reason
- Approver
- ARR impact
- Churn/downsell reason
