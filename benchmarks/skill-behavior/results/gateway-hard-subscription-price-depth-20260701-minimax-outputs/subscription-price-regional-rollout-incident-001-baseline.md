# Decision Memo: Regional Price Harmonization Rollout — Tranche 2 Go/No-Go

**To:** Pricing Council, GTM Leadership, Legal, Finance
**From:** Monetization Strategy
**Date:** [T]
**Recommendation:** **PAUSE** Tranche 2 rollout. **DO NOT** rollback Tranche 1. **CHANGE** the rollout design before resuming.

---

## 1. Situation Summary

Tranche 1 (T1) revenue uplift is positive on paper, but four signals are degrading trust:

| Signal | Direction | Segments Most Affected |
|---|---|---|
| Voluntary cancellations | ↑ materially | Grandfathered, Education |
| Refund requests | ↑↑ materially | Discounted, Enterprise-contract |
| Social complaints | ↑ (above threshold) | All four |
| Sales escalations | ↑ | Enterprise-contract (renewal risk) |

The pattern is consistent with **contract / expectation violations**, not price elasticity. A simple "continue" or "rollback" is the wrong frame; the issue is **rollout design and segment handling**, not the strategic direction.

---

## 2. Decision

**Pause Tranche 2 for 30–45 days. Repair Tranche 1. Redesign before resuming.**

Rationale:
- **Rollback** destroys T1 uplift (sunk), signals instability, and won't fix root causes that will recur.
- **Continue** compounds churn in high-LTV segments (Education, Enterprise) where switching/advocacy costs are asymmetric.
- **Pause + repair + redesign** preserves strategic intent while protecting revenue at risk and brand.

---

## 3. Tranche 2 Go/No-Go Criteria (gates)

Resume Tranche 2 only when **all** are true:

- [ ] T1 cancellation rate returns to within 1.5× of pre-T1 baseline.
- [ ] Refund/chargeback rate ≤ pre-T1 baseline + 1pp.
- [ ] Zero open complaints from Education or Enterprise accounts.
- [ ] Legal sign-off on revised disclosure language.
- [ ] Grandfathered and contract customers formally re-acknowledged.
- [ ] Tax-inclusive pricing math re-certified by Finance per locale.

---

## 4. Recovery Plan

### A. Immediate (0–7 days) — Stabilize

1. **Freeze** any in-flight pricing communications; route all pricing objections to a single war-room.
2. **Grandfather honor:** lock existing subscribers at current price for ≥12 months, opt-in to new pricing.
3. **Enterprise-contract audit:** identify every account with papered pricing; issue side-letter affirming contract terms supersede new list pricing. Sales-led, Legal-reviewed.
4. **Education:** reinstate education-tier pricing where verification is current; offer a 1-time goodwill credit (e.g., 1 month) to verified affected users.
5. **Refund triage:** auto-approve refunds for customers citing price change as reason; SLA 5 business days.
6. **Social response playbook:** templated, empathetic, non-defensive reply within 24h; do not delete or argue.

### B. Short-Term (7–30 days) — Repair & Learn

7. **Customer outreach:** personalized email to all T1 affected accounts explaining what changed, what didn't, and what's being fixed. Owner: CSM for Enterprise, lifecycle email for SMB/Education.
8. **Root-cause analysis:** publish internal RCA distinguishing (a) legitimate price-hike complaints from (b) mis-execution failures (poor disclosure, tax math errors, broken discounts).
9. **Billing reconciliation:** Finance to identify and credit any tax-inclusive miscalculations; document for audit.
10. **CX instrumentation:** instrument cancellation, refund, NPS, and support volume by segment and country; weekly review.

### C. Structural (30–90 days) — Redesign

11. **Segment-tiered rollout matrix** before resuming:
    - *Grandfathered:* honor existing price; migrate on renewal only.
    - *Education:* always maintain a verified-discount tier; never fold into list.
    - *Enterprise (papered):* contract-first; no unilateral change.
    - *Discounted promo:* honor through promo end; bridge credit if uplift > X%.
    - *New/standard:* harmonize per market plan.
12. **Minimum 60-day notice** for any list-price change in any new market.
13. **Tax-inclusive disclosure:** locale-specific, plain-language; required at checkout and in pre-renewal notice.
14. **Change-management gate:** every future tranche requires Pricing + Legal + CS + Country GM sign-off with documented segment impact assessment.

---

## 5. Rollout Redesign (for Tranche 2 onward)

| Element | Old approach | New approach |
|---|---|---|
| Sequencing | Country-led, all segments at once | Segment-then-country matrix |
| Contracts | Implicit | Explicit grandfather + side-letters |
| Notice | 30 days | 60 days + in-product banner |
| Tax presentation | Region-defaulted | Locale-specific, pre-checkout |
| Education | Folded into list | Permanent verified tier |
| Escalation path | Support → Sales | War-room → CSM → Account exec |
| Rollback trigger | None defined | Defined thresholds (see §6) |

---

## 6. Decision Triggers

- **Continue recovery:** metrics trending toward gates within 30 days.
- **Extend pause:** metrics flat or worsening at day 30.
- **Rollback T1** (last resort): if (a) refund/chargeback rate exceeds 3× baseline, (b) Enterprise churn >5% in T1 cohort, or (c) regulatory action threatened.
- **Cancel harmonization entirely:** only if redesign cannot satisfy §3 gates without eliminating strategic uplift.

---

## 7. Governance & Reporting

- **Weekly** Pricing Council review of the six metrics in §3.
- **Bi-weekly** exec update to CFO + CRO.
- **Single owner:** VP Monetization, with RACI: Pricing (A), CS (R), Sales (R), Legal (C), Finance (C), Country GMs (I).
- **Audit trail:** every customer-facing communication, every exception, every credit logged in a dedicated tracker.

---

## 8. What This Memo Does Not Decide

- Final T1 list prices (Finance owns).
- Whether to publish an apology externally (Comms owns; recommendation: targeted, not broadcast).
- Long-term harmonization strategy (Pricing Council owns).

**Bottom line:** Pause, repair, redesign. The strategy isn't broken — the rollout was.
