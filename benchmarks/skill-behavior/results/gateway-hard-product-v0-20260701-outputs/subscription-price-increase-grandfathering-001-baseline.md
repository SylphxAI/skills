## Subscription Price Increase Plan (B2B SaaS)

### 1. Objectives and Guardrails
**Goals**
- Increase ARR while protecting net revenue retention.
- Move customers toward current packaging and usage-based economics.
- Minimize churn, surprise, and support escalations.

**Guardrails**
- No retroactive price changes.
- Honor signed contract terms.
- Provide clear notice before renewal or billing change.
- Maintain documented exception approvals.
- Track churn, downgrade, contraction, and expansion separately.

---

## 2. Customer Segmentation

| Segment | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Monthly self-serve users | Price increase on next billing cycle after required notice period |
| Legacy annual contracts | Increase applies at renewal only |
| Enterprise contracts | Handled by account team during renewal cycle |
| Grandfathered plans | Migrate to current plans or apply capped increase |
| High-usage / overage customers | Review overage economics and offer usage bundles |
| High churn-risk accounts | Targeted retention offer or phased increase |
| Strategic / reference customers | Sales leadership review before change |

---

## 3. Pricing Policy

### Standard Increase
- Apply a baseline increase, e.g. **8–15%**, depending on plan and market benchmark.
- Higher increases may apply to severely underpriced legacy plans.
- Usage overages should be aligned to current list pricing unless contractually protected.

### Annual Contracts
- Existing annual contracts remain unchanged until renewal.
- Renewal quote includes new pricing at least **60–90 days before renewal**.
- If auto-renewal language requires notice, follow the contractual notice window.

### Monthly Self-Serve
- Notify at least **30 days before price change**.
- Apply on the first billing date after the notice period.
- Offer annual prepay option to lock current or discounted pricing for 12 months.

### Enterprise Customers
- Account owner communicates change.
- Renewal proposal should include:
  - New price
  - Business justification
  - Usage trend
  - Value delivered
  - Migration path
  - Any phased option

---

## 4. Grandfathered Plan Strategy

### Options
1. **Migrate to current packaging**
   - Recommended default.
   - Provide feature mapping and commercial comparison.

2. **Grandfathered plan uplift**
   - Apply capped increase, e.g. 10–20%.
   - Set sunset date for old plan.

3. **Phased migration**
   - Year 1: partial increase.
   - Year 2: full current price.
   - Best for large or high-risk accounts.

### Required Decision
Grandfathered plans should not remain indefinitely. Set a formal sunset date, e.g. **12–18 months**, except for approved enterprise exceptions.

---

## 5. Usage Overage Treatment

- Keep contracted included usage unchanged until renewal.
- At renewal, align overage pricing to current rate card.
- For customers with repeated overages:
  - Offer larger committed tier.
  - Offer prepaid usage bundle.
  - Offer enterprise plan if usage is material.
- Avoid surprise invoices by sending usage alerts at 75%, 90%, and 100% of included usage.

---

## 6. Migration Offers

| Customer Type | Offer |
|---|---|
| Monthly self-serve | Lock current price for 12 months by switching to annual |
| Legacy annual | Renewal discount if migrating to current plan |
| High-risk customer | Phased increase or temporary discount |
| Heavy usage customer | Bundle discount for committed usage |
| Enterprise | Multi-year agreement with capped annual uplift |

Example offers:
- “Move to annual by [date] and lock your current rate for 12 months.”
- “Migrate to the current plan at renewal and receive a 10% transition discount for year one.”
- “Commit to a higher usage tier and reduce overage exposure.”

---

## 7. Notification Timing

| Event | Timing |
|---|---|
| Internal enablement | 2–3 weeks before customer notice |
| Monthly customer notice | Minimum 30 days before billing impact |
| Annual renewal notice | 60–90 days before renewal |
| Enterprise account planning | 120 days before renewal |
| Final billing reminder | 7–14 days before effective date |
| In-app/banner notice | During notice window |

All notices should include:
- Current price
- New price
- Effective date
- Reason for change
- Customer options
- Support/contact path
- Cancellation or renewal terms, if applicable

## 8. Customer Messaging

### Core Message
“We are updating pricing to reflect continued product investment, infrastructure costs, support improvements, and expanded platform value. Your new price will take effect on [date]. Your current contract terms remain honored through the end of your current term.”

### Self-Serve Email

[Company]

---

## 9. Support Script

### If customer asks why
“We’ve expanded the product and continue investing in security, reliability, support, and new capabilities. The new pricing better reflects the value and cost of delivering the service.”

### If customer threatens cancellation
“I understand. I can review your usage and plan fit to see whether there is a better option, including annual billing, a migration offer, or a right-sized plan.”

### If customer says they were grandfathered
“Your legacy terms have been honored to date. We are now transitioning legacy plans toward current packaging. Your options are [A], [B], or [C].”

### If customer asks for an exception
“I can submit an exception request. Approval depends on contract terms, account history, usage, renewal timing, and business impact.”

---

## 10. Sales Exception Policy

Exceptions require documented approval.

| Exception Type | Approval |
|---|---|
| Up to 10% discount | Account executive / manager |
| 10–20% discount | Sales director |
| Phased increase | VP Sales or RevOps |
| Grandfather extension | CRO approval |
| Strategic account exception | CRO + CFO |
| Non-standard legal terms | Legal approval |

Each exception must include:
- Customer ARR
- Current and proposed price
- Renewal date
- Usage level
- Churn risk
- Competitive risk
- Requested concession
- Expiration date

---

## 11. Cancellation Risk Management

Create risk tiers:
- **Low risk:** standard notice and pricing.
- **Medium risk:** proactive email plus support follow-up.
- **High risk:** account owner outreach and tailored offer.
- **Critical:** executive sponsor review.

Risk factors:
- Recent support issues
- Low product usage
- Large increase percentage
- Month-to-month billing
- Negative NPS
- Open renewal negotiation
- Competitive evaluation

---
