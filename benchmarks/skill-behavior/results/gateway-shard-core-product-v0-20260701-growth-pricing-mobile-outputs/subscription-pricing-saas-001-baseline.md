## B2B Analytics SaaS Subscription Pricing

### 1. Packaging Overview

| Plan | Target Customer | Price | Seats Included | Usage Included | Best For |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Free Trial | Evaluation | $0 for 14 days | 5 seats | 100k events, 5 dashboards | Product validation |
| Team | SMB / departmental teams | $1,200/month | 10 seats | 5M events/month | Growing analytics teams |
| Enterprise | Mid-market / large orgs | Custom, starting at $5,000/month | 25 seats | 25M events/month | Multi-team or mission-critical analytics |

Annual billing receives a **15% discount**.  
Enterprise annual contracts are preferred and may include ramp pricing.

## 2. Free Trial

### Terms
- **14-day free trial**
- No credit card required
- Includes:
  - 5 users
  - 100k events
  - 5 dashboards
  - 2 data connectors
  - Community/email support
- Excludes:
  - SSO/SAML
  - SLA commitments
  - Custom data retention
  - Production procurement/security reviews

### Trial Conversion Goal
The trial should prove:
- Data connection completed
- First dashboard created
- At least 2 active users
- Clear usage path to paid plan

Recommended product-led conversion trigger:  
Prompt upgrade when customer reaches **80% of event limit**, adds the 6th user, or requests production features.

## 5. Procurement Requirements

### Team Plan
Designed for low-friction purchasing.

Requirements:
- Self-serve checkout or invoice
- Credit card or ACH
- Standard terms of service
- No custom MSA unless annual contract exceeds $25k
- Security documentation available via self-serve portal

### Enterprise Plan
Designed for formal procurement.

Requirements:
- Annual contract
- MSA and order form
- Security questionnaire support
- DPA available
- SOC 2 report under NDA
- Vendor onboarding support
- Purchase order accepted
- Net 30 payment terms standard
- Net 45 or Net 60 only with finance approval
- Legal redlines allowed only above $60k ARR

### Procurement Guardrails
- Custom terms require approval from legal and finance
- Non-standard SLA requires approval from product/engineering
- Unlimited usage should not be sold without caps or fair-use language
- Discounts above 20% require executive approval

## 7. Expansion Path

Recommended customer growth path:

1. **Free Trial**
   - Validate data ingestion and dashboards

2. **Team**
   - Convert when team needs production analytics
   - Common triggers:
     - More than 5 seats
     - More than 100k events
     - More than 5 dashboards

3. **Expanded Team**
   - Add seats and event capacity
   - Trigger Enterprise review at:
     - 25+ seats
     - 20M+ monthly events
     - Security requirements
     - SSO/SAML need
     - Procurement involvement

4. **Enterprise**
   - Standardize across departments
   - Add governance, SSO, audit logs, SLA, retention

5. **Strategic Enterprise**
   - Multi-year contract
   - Dedicated CSM
   - Custom retention
   - Volume event pricing
   - Executive business reviews

## 8. Unit Economics Concerns

### Pricing Must Protect Gross Margin
Primary cost drivers:
- Data ingestion volume
- Query compute
- Data storage and retention
- Dashboard refresh frequency
- Customer support load
- Implementation services
- Security/procurement effort

Recommended gross margin targets:
- Team: **80%+**
- Enterprise software subscription: **75–85%**
- Professional services: **30–50%**, or priced to break even strategically

### Guardrails
- Do not offer unlimited events without fair-use limits
- Overages should cover marginal infrastructure cost plus margin
- Retention extensions should be monetized
- Heavy implementation should be billed separately
- Enterprise discounts should be tied to:
  - Annual prepay
  - Multi-year term
  - Minimum usage commitment
  - Expansion commitment

### Metrics to Monitor
| Metric | Target |
|---|---:|
| CAC payback | <12 months SMB, <18 months Enterprise |
| Gross margin | 75–85% |
| Net revenue retention | 110%+ |
| Logo churn | <10% annually |
| Trial-to-paid conversion | 15–25% |
| Support cost as % of ARR | <10% |

---

## 9. Recommended Final Price Architecture

- Use **seat-based pricing** for buyer familiarity
- Use **event-based usage pricing** to protect infrastructure margin
- Use **Enterprise packaging** for governance, security, SLA, and procurement needs
- Keep Team self-serve and low-friction
- Use annual discounts to improve cash flow and retention
- Treat onboarding, premium support, retention, and heavy usage as monetizable expansion levers
